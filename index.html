About

I recently completed my Ph.D. in the School of Computer Science at Peking University, advised by Prof. Yingfei Xiong. During my Ph.D., my research focused on program generation, program understanding, pre-trained large models, and natural language processing. I developed several novel techniques for neural program repair, code generation, and code retrieval, which were published in top-tier conferences such as ICSE, IJCAI, and AAAI. My work on type-aware neural program repair and syntax-guided code generation has significantly advanced the state-of-the-art in automated software engineering.

I obtained my B.S. degree in Computer Science from Peking University in 2019, where I laid a solid foundation in software engineering, machine learning, and programming languages. During my undergraduate studies, I actively participated in research projects and competitions, which sparked my passion for solving real-world problems through cutting-edge technologies.

Currently, I am working at DeepSeek, a leading company in artificial intelligence and large-scale language models. My role involves researching and developing advanced large models for code generation and logical reasoning. Specifically, I focus on designing models that can generate high-quality, syntactically correct code from natural language descriptions, as well as models capable of performing complex logical reasoning tasks. My work aims to bridge the gap between human intent and machine execution, enabling more efficient and intelligent software development.

At DeepSeek, I collaborate with a talented team of researchers and engineers to push the boundaries of what large models can achieve in the domains of programming and reasoning. I am particularly interested in exploring how pre-trained models can be fine-tuned for specific tasks, such as automated bug fixing, code summarization, and program synthesis. My ultimate goal is to create models that can assist developers in writing better code, reducing errors, and improving productivity.
